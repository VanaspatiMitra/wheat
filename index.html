About the Crop:

PBW 343 is a popular wheat variety known for its high yield and disease resistance, particularly against rust. It thrives in well-drained loamy soil with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. The average per acre production is around 22-25 quintals under optimal conditions. Water needs vary by growth stage, typically requiring 4-6 irrigations (about 450-550 mm of water). Profit margins are generally good due to its high productivity and low susceptibility to diseases, making it a preferred choice for farmers in regions with moderate to high rainfall.

  • Soil Composition: well-drained loamy soil witha pH level between 6.0 and 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 1.48 to 1.80 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Disease Susceptibility, Water Stress, Pest Attacks
  • Fertilizers: Phosphorus, Zinc Sulphate, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Imidacloprid, Chlorpyrifos, Spinosad, Mancozeb
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹19,600 to ₹40,000
